## Source Notes

The broader Japanese Color Atlas project combines public-domain, open-access, scholarly, and manually curated references. This free repository only contains sample exports intended for design and development workflow testing.

Key source categories used by the full project include:

- Traditional Japanese color names tied to dye plants, mineral pigments, textiles, and historical eras
- Tobunken (Tokyo National Research Institute for Cultural Properties) color-name attestations where applicable
- Seasonal (koyomi / 72-season) and kasane (襲の色目) layered-color references
- Public references for color-name etymology and cultural context

Each record in the full atlas is labeled with a source-status tier (source-backed, attributed, measured, disputed). HEX values are screen approximations of historically attested color names, never claimed as exact colorimetry.

## Data Boundaries

- Hex color values are facts and may be used freely.
- Color-name attestations and cultural context come from referenced sources in the broader project.
- Research summaries and structured exports should preserve attribution to Japanese Color Atlas and relevant upstream sources.
- This repository does not include complete 144-color paid resource packs, full Figma Variables, full AI prompts, full Procreate/ASE bundles, or full research JSON datasets.
